Travel to Las Vegas

The Groom's Travel to Las Vegas (Dave)

Purchased Southwest tickets in advance.

Winter storm Elliot.

Was notified by Anke that my flight was apparently canceled (Anke, can you dig up the WhatsApp messages where you notified me and send a screenshot?).

I changed my SWA flight to 12/28.

Anke picked up a Frontier Airlines flight, and purchased one checked bag.

Frontier has the most confusing website, ever.  I purchased "The Works", which was advertised at $88 but actually cost $35 because of Anke's earlier purchase of the checked bag.  This seemed to be the easiest way to get a carry-on bag.

The flight was very late.  The stated reason was flight crew work hour restrictions.  It actually departed around 12:30 a.m.

The staff at the gate were somewhat rude, and stated very plainly that carry-ons cost and had to be purchased, and if you boarded the plane with a carry-on you didn't pay for, it was going to cost you even more.  The tone was nothing less than parochial and threatening.

The captain showed up.  Smartly dressed, tall, caucasian, perhaps 55, a lot of gray, a few extra pounds.  Walked in a straight line.  Seemed very happy and energetic.  Was making smalltalk with the gate crew and cabin crew.  Inspired confidence, especially with being apparently sober and very awake.  I did not notice the FO.

Deicing took a while.  The captain was very careful to explain what was happening in a non-alarmist manner ("be sure it is safe to operate the aircraft" rather than "so we don't crash").  A few trucks spraying on liquids.

Completely rude cabin crew.  Parochial and threatening.  "You've been assigned a specific seat, and we expect you to sit in that seat.  You must obtain permission from a crew member to swap or change seats."

Captain even made light of the airline's a la carte policies.  "We realize you had to make a lot of choices to be sitting in the seat, and we thank you for your business."

Seats had the smallest tray ever (get a screenshot from my video), yet they still required it be upright for takeoff and landing.

Seats did not recline.

Closer examination of the seats revealed that they were at the stuctural minimum.  Rectangular aluminum tube around the perimeter, with a shell that consumed the depth of the tube but no more.  Designed to fit the maximum number of people in the minimum space.

Had a guy that I believe was 50-ish and Polish and didn't speak English to my left, dressed in athletic/workout clothes.  Gave him half my orange juice. During the flight, we were both trying to find a comfortable position to sleep, and we could not.  The gal in the window seat had it easy because she had a corner to lean into.  The Polish guy and I were both miserable.  I finally dozed off with my head resting forward against the seat in front of me.

Frontier is the worst airline ever in every category except getting me there.  Confusing website, a la carte policies, hostile gate crew, hostile cabin crew, awful seating.

Received numerous marketing e-mails after the trip advertising things like $19 fares from Detroit.  Presumably checked bags, carry-on bags, and air to breathe purchased separately.

Canceled my outbound SWA flight.  The website allowed me to do that.

Southwest got a lot of bad press, and had a "meltdown".

My credit as a result of canceling the outbound flight never expire, and I got 25,000 points as well, that never expire.  I would say that Southwest compensated me fairly, all things considered.

Was on The Deuce to find the marriage license office when got a WhatsApp message from Anke indicating she was through immigration.  At that point exited the bus and took a cab to the airport.

Was engaged in a fascinating conversation with a guy who was also an inconvenienced Southwest customer, and was born and raised in rural Missouri.  We discussed pilot's licenses, and it ends up that he earned a license perhaps 3 decades before I did and wanted to become an aviator in the Vietnam War, but the war ended and the air force no longer had a need for pilots.  We discussed how expensive it is to become an airline pilot if you do it as a civilian, and also the high number of mass shootings in the U.S., which neither of us had an explanation for.  Fascinating the people you meet on buses.

Anke and I took the shuttle to pick up the rental car.

Then went to Excalibur then took the bus to the marriage license office.

The Groom's Thoughts and Notes on The Hoover Dam (Dave)

I had originally thought of The Hoover Dam as an optional stop, but at Anke's urging, we stopped.

There was a security checkpoint where they asked about drones and firearms, and they looked at the back seat of the car.  This is something I don't remember from years past, perhaps because drones have become more of an issue.

The parking was in a deck surrounded by rock.  My favorite parking deck in the world.

We saw the film.  Impressive.

We went on the power plant tour.  We were fortunate that there were generator rotors out for maintenance.  Very  impressive.

Reusable aluminum water bottles with cold water out of a vending machine for about $5 each.  Best deal ever.  Lost mine, unfortunately.
