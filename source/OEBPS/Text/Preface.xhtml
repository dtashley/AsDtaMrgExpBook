The Preface, a Work in Progress:

In 2020, we (Anke and Dave) reconnected after about 20 years, fell in love, and decided to marry.

That story, in and of itself, would not have been especially remarkable or made an interesting book.

But there is more.  There are attorneys.  And more attorneys.  Did we mention the attorneys?  Old movies would have you believe that getting married is as simple as convincing the local preacher to "hitch" you, tying string and cans to your car, and driving off towards happiness and success.  We did find that the process had a few more steps, at least in our case.

At the time we became engaged, Dave resided in the United States (Allen Park, Michigan), but Anke was a German citizen living in Istanbul, Turkey. Since we had decided to live together in the United States, there was the complexity of the U.S. immigration process; involving forms, documents, translators, translator's fees, procedures, interviews, government fees, attorneys, attorney fees, notaries, and lots of delay.

An attorney assisted us with the immigration process. (Attorney count so far:  1.)

Additionally, at the time we became engaged, we had both individually amassed substantial assets and each of us had our own investing style. A prenuptial agreement was prudent to prevent our individual assets from automatically becoming marital property, so we could have a marriage with a great deal of independent financial decision-making. The prenuptial agreement itself was an interesting journey involving attorneys, attorney fees, notaries, and remote notarization procedures.

The process of drafting and executing a prenuptial agreement that will be recognized by a court in Michigan requires two attorneys—one for each partner. (Attorney count so far:  3.)

Finally, we both required an attorney to assist with wills and estate planning.  (Final attorney count:  4.)

The wedding itself was straightforward, but behind the scenes we had an entourage of four very necessary (and moderately expensive) attorneys. The title of the book almost wrote itself.

It would be fair to characterize this book as an unconventional (übernerd) love story, combined with a practical description of the nuts and bolts of immigration, a prenuptial agreement, and estate planning as we experienced them.

This book would likely have two primary audiences:

  • Couples contemplating marriage and/or immigration who might find our experiences of interest.
  • Readers seeking an unusual read at a low price.

This book is not intended to provide legal advice, and is not a substitute for legal advice from an attorney.  This book documents our experiences with immigration, prenuptial agreements, and estate planning; as well as our understanding of the law.  Our experiences may not be representative, laws may vary from state to state, and certainly our legal understanding may be flawed.  Please consult an attorney!

Because there are really two separate stories to tell, from two points of view, most sections of the book were written exclusively by either Anke or Dave.  Sections of the book that were written by only one author identify the author by including either "(Anke)" or "(Dave)" in the section title.

We both invite correspondence about this book.  We would be glad to correct any errors in the book, and we would also be glad to include opinions that are different than our own.  Our editing and re-release cycle is annual, so there may be a delay of up to approximately a year until corrections or additions appear in the next edition of the book.
